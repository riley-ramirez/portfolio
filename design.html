Bye Bye Birdies

Six years after scientists reported that billions of birds vanished from the North American landscape since 1970, conservationists are still racing to reverse catastrophic losses that show no sign of slowing down.

This project was part of my 9-month-long master's capstone at the UC Berkeley Graduate School of Journalism. I talked to dozens of scientists and experts to understand why grassland bird populations have declined so dramatically, and even got to travel to New Mexico to witness researchers monitoring and tagging grassland birds in the Chihuahuan Desert first hand. As part of my reporting, I captured film in New Mexico using a Canon C100 camera and DJI Pocket Osmo III and analyzed three different federal datasets using R. After gathering all of my reporting, I used Figma to design the layout and visual elements of the story, and then used Adobe After Effects, Premiere Pro, Illustrator and Procreate to edit the videos, animations and hand-drawn illustrations and data visualizations. Using a combination of HTML, CSS and JavaScript in a Svelte framework, I built the interactive web page from scratch and hosted it on GitHub Pages.

Is Moneyball Dead?

As the payroll gap continues to widen, small-market teams struggle to remain competitive.

This project was the first interactive story I ever built as part of my J222 Interactive Narratives course. Our assignment was to use data or other multimedia elements to enhance the storytelling experience on a desktop layout. In my team, I pitched the story and was responsible for writing the majority of the narrative content, as well as designing and building the web page using HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I designed the hero image, Shohei Ohtani award graphic and scatterplot data visuualizations in Canva, and created the motion graphic video of where teams who had made it to the MLB playoffs in the last decade fell on the payroll distribution spread.
